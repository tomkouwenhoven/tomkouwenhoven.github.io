Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at Leiden University interested in [Hybrid Intelligence](https://www.hybrid-intelligence-centre.nl/). My research focuses on the social reasoning abilities, such as Theory of Mind, of computational models like Large Language Models. This work is carried out together with [Max van Duijn](https://www.mjvd.nl/), [Rineke Verbrugge](https://rinekeverbrugge.nl/), and [Daniel Balliet](https://amsterdamcooperationlab.com/danielballiet/). 

I obtained my PhD in 2025 at the [Leiden Institute for Advanced Computer Science (LIACS)](https://liacs.leidenuniv.nl/) and  [Creative Intelligence Lab](https://cil.liacs.nl/). My PhD project, entitled ‘Collaborative Meaning-Making: The Emergence of Novel Languages in Humans, Machines, and Human-Machine Interactions’, supervised by [Tessa Verhoef](https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/en/staffmembers/tessa-verhoef#tab-1), [Roy de Kleijn](https://roydekleijn.nl/), and [Stephan Raaijmakers](https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/medewerkers/stephan-raaijmakers#tab-1), aimed to improve communication between humans and Artificial Intelligence by co-creating shared vocabularies. Doing so, I unravelled the mechanisms required to create mutual understanding about words and their corresponding meanings such that both humans and computers understand what is meant by the current communicative message. This project took inspiration from the field of Language Evolution and used adaptive machine-learning algorithms and contemporary Large Language Models to facilitate natural interactions between humans and machines.
